## 1. Executive Summary

NixIt should launch as the anti-solo quit product: a lightweight social commitment for people who already know they want to stop vaping, but keep failing when the plan stays private. The campaign goal is not broad awareness first. It is high-intent conversion among 18-24 users who already feel friction, shame, or fatigue around repeat nicotine use and are actively looking for a real quit date.

The 90-day plan uses one core message across every channel: **Don't quit alone.** The funnel pairs emotionally direct organic content with proof-heavy paid creative, then turns new users into referral fuel through monthly cohort rituals, UGC prompts, and visible celebration moments.

Primary 90-day targets:

- 12,000 landing page visitors
- 3,000 account signups
- 1,200 cohort joins
- 40% signup-to-enrollment rate
- Blended CAC below $22
- 25% of new joins influenced by creator, referral, or cohort sharing loops
- Week-4 cohort retention above 60%

Budget recommendation: $24,000 total.

- $12,000 paid social
- $6,000 creator seeding and ambassador incentives
- $3,000 referral rewards and cohort event prizes
- $2,000 content production
- $1,000 tooling, analytics, and contingency

Paid versus organic mix:

- Weeks 1-4: 35% paid, 65% organic and creator seeding
- Weeks 5-9: 50% paid, 50% organic
- Weeks 10-13: 60% paid, 40% organic, focused on highest-converting audiences and retargeting

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement

For young nicotine users who are tired of promising themselves they will quit "soon," NixIt is the quit-together app that gives them a real date, a real cohort, and real accountability so they do not have to rely on willpower alone.

### Category

Peer-accountability nicotine quitting app.

### Core Promise

You do not need to quit alone.

### Differentiators

- Shared monthly Nix Date instead of vague self-starting
- Small cohorts instead of anonymous feeds
- Shared timer and progress instead of private streaks
- Group chat built for cravings and check-ins
- Tap-out rule that turns quitting the app into a visible support moment

### Message Hierarchy

1. Don't quit alone.
2. Pick a date you can't ghost.
3. Join a cohort that is quitting with you.
4. Use shared progress and chat to stay in it.
5. If you want out, the group sees it and can help.

### Reasons To Believe

- Quitting alone already failed many users multiple times.
- Public commitment increases follow-through.
- Group identity lowers the isolation of nicotine withdrawal.
- Shared rituals create momentum around a hard behavior change.

## 3. Customer Personas

### Persona 1: Campus Restarter

- Age: 18-22
- Situation: College student who hits nicotine most during social downtime and stress
- Trigger: Feels gross, distracted, or broke and wants a clean reset before the next month starts
- Why they join: Wants structure without a clinical vibe
- Barrier: Worries strangers will be weird or intense
- Best message: "Start with people who are done saying someday."

### Persona 2: Repeat Quitter

- Age: 19-24
- Situation: Has already tried solo quitting two or more times
- Trigger: Frustrated by relapse pattern and tired of self-negotiation
- Why they join: Knows accountability is missing
- Barrier: Afraid of failing publicly
- Best message: "You already tried private willpower. Try shared momentum."

### Persona 3: Quiet Professional

- Age: 22-24
- Situation: Early-career worker hiding nicotine use between tasks or commutes
- Trigger: Wants better energy and less dependence
- Why they join: Wants simple, mobile, non-preachy support
- Barrier: Thinks cohort products are too time-consuming
- Best message: "A small group, not a second job."

### Persona 4: Concerned Influencer Circle

- Age: Secondary audience
- Situation: Parents, school wellness staff, mentors, resident advisors
- Trigger: Wants a shareable tool for someone ready to quit
- Why they join: Sees value in structured peer support
- Barrier: Needs confidence the product is supportive, not shaming
- Best message: "A social support system for the moment someone is finally ready."

## 4. Messaging Framework

### Psychological Hooks

- Commitment device: the Nix Date makes the promise concrete
- Social proof: people like me are doing this right now
- Identity shift: I am part of a quitting cohort, not a solo struggler
- Friction on relapse: leaving requires visibility, which creates pause
- Belonging: cravings feel less isolating when the group is active
- Fresh-start effect: the first of the month gives the behavior change a natural reset point

### Emotional Pillars

- Relief: "I do not have to white-knuckle this alone"
- Resolve: "This time has a date and witnesses"
- Belonging: "Other people are in the same hard week"
- Momentum: "The group makes it easier to keep going one more day"

### Objection Handling

- "I can quit by myself": If that was enough, you would not still be searching.
- "I do not want a preachy app": NixIt is light, direct, and social.
- "I do not want strangers in my business": The cohort is small and focused on one goal.
- "What if I slip": The product is built for hard moments, not perfection theater.

### Motivation Framework By Journey Stage

- Aware: "Quitting alone keeps turning into later."
- Consideration: "This gives me a date and a group."
- Signup: "I can join next month in under two minutes."
- Activation: "I see my cohort, my timer, and the chat immediately."
- Retention: "The group expects me to show up."
- Referral: "Send this to the friend who keeps saying next month."

## 5. Platform Strategy

### TikTok

- Role: Discovery, emotional resonance, confession-style storytelling, creator proof
- Creative style: Face-to-camera honesty, comments turned into content, hard-cut edits, screenshots of chats and countdowns
- KPI focus: CTR, profile visits, landing page clicks, saves, shares

### Instagram

- Role: Credibility, visual identity, carousel education, Reels for proof and reminders, Stories for countdowns and polls
- Creative style: Clean minimal graphics, social proof screenshots, short testimonial-style clips
- KPI focus: Saves, story taps, DMs, profile visits, bio link clicks

### Snapchat

- Role: High-frequency reminders, story intimacy, short-form urgency, campus ambassador amplification
- Creative style: Native vertical story sequences, polls, stickers, fast selfies, low-production authenticity
- KPI focus: Swipe-ups, replies, story completion rate

### Creator and Ambassador Layer

- Role: Borrow trust from real nicotine quitters, student life creators, and humor pages
- Structure: 20 nano creators, 10 micro creators, 12 campus ambassadors over 90 days
- KPI focus: cost per signup, creator-specific conversion, content reuse rate in paid ads

### Community Layer

- Role: Keep activation and retention high after signup
- Channels: in-app chat, email nudges, optional Discord waitlist room, cohort celebration posts
- KPI focus: day-1 activation, day-7 participation, day-30 retention, referral share rate

## 19. Risk Analysis

### Risk: Platform policy sensitivity around nicotine content

- Mitigation: avoid glamorizing nicotine, avoid minors in paid targeting, position around quitting support and accountability

### Risk: Product promise feels too harsh because of tap-out rule

- Mitigation: explain it as a support moment, not punishment

### Risk: Low cohort density hurts product experience

- Mitigation: focus spend around monthly join windows, hold waitlist if necessary, consolidate small cohorts

### Risk: Creator content becomes repetitive

- Mitigation: rotate formats across confession, skit, demo, duet, countdown, and screenshot proof

### Risk: Early users fear judgment

- Mitigation: keep tone hopeful, normalize relapse fear, show supportive chat examples

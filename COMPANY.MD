# NixIt Company Overview

## What NixIt Is

NixIt is a cohort-based nicotine quitting product. It turns quitting from a private, solo effort into a shared commitment made with a small group of people who are all stopping at the same time.

Instead of asking someone to rely only on willpower, NixIt gives them a quit date, a peer group, a shared sense of progress, and a lightweight social structure that keeps them accountable.

The current product is a mobile-friendly web app.

## The Core Idea

NixIt is built around a simple belief: people are more likely to follow through on a hard behavior change when they do it with others.

Users do not just sign up for an app. They join a cohort tied to a specific monthly "Nix Date". That date is the day the group starts quitting together. From there, the product gives them a shared dashboard, a visible timer, and group chat so the experience feels communal rather than isolating.

## The Problem It Solves

Most quitting tools are built around individual tracking, individual motivation, and individual relapse management. That works for some people, but it leaves a major gap for users who need social pressure, shared momentum, and peer support.

NixIt is designed to solve for:

- people who want accountability, not just information,
- people who struggle to quit alone,
- people who are motivated by belonging to a group,
- people who benefit from a clear start date instead of an open-ended promise to quit "soon".

## Who It Is For

NixIt is primarily for young people, including high-school-age users, who want to quit nicotine in a structured but socially familiar way.

More broadly, it is for:

- anyone trying to stop vaping or using nicotine,
- users who prefer peer support over clinical or highly formal programs,
- people who respond well to group accountability,
- users who want a simpler experience than a full health or habit-tracking platform.

It is not positioned as a medical treatment platform. It is a behavior-change and accountability product.

## What the Product Does

At a product level, NixIt does four things:

1. It helps a user commit to a specific quit date.
2. It places that user into a small quitting cohort.
3. It keeps the cohort connected through a shared progress experience.
4. It makes accountability visible, social, and time-based.

## How the Website Works

The website experience is straightforward.

### 1. Account creation

A user creates an account with email and password, chooses a username, and can add a profile photo.

### 2. Cohort enrollment

After signing up, the user chooses a Nix Date. Each Nix Date corresponds to a monthly cohort, typically starting on the first of the month. Users join one cohort at a time.

### 3. Shared dashboard

Once enrolled, the user lands in a dashboard that shows:

- their active cohort,
- how long the cohort has been nicotine-free,
- how many members are in the cohort,
- who else is participating,
- quick access to the cohort conversation.

### 4. Cohort chat

Users can talk with the other people in their cohort. The chat is there to make check-ins, encouragement, and hard moments visible to the group.

### 5. Leaving a cohort

If a user wants to exit, they do not simply leave instantly. NixIt uses a structured "tap out" mechanic.

A user can only be in one cohort at a time. To leave that cohort, a majority of the active users in the cohort must agree to let them out. Once released, the user can join another cohort.

This mechanic is intentional. It gives the cohort a chance to step in and support someone at the exact moment they are most likely to want to quit the quitting process itself. Instead of disappearing silently during a hard moment, the user has to surface that moment to the group, which creates one more opportunity for encouragement, intervention, and accountability.

## What Makes NixIt Different

NixIt is not just a tracker, and it is not just a chat app.

Its differentiation comes from combining:

- a fixed start date,
- a small-group structure,
- visible shared progress,
- accountability through participation,
- a tap-out rule that requires cohort approval,
- a product flow built around quitting together rather than consuming content alone.

The product is intentionally narrow. It does not try to be a broad wellness platform. It focuses on one use case: helping people quit nicotine with the support and pressure of a cohort.

## The Product Experience in One Sentence

NixIt helps people quit nicotine by letting them join a monthly peer cohort, start together on the same quit date, and stay accountable through shared progress and conversation.

## Current Product Scope

The website currently centers on the core quitting flow:

- signup and login,
- profile creation,
- cohort selection,
- one active cohort per user,
- dashboard with a shared nicotine-free timer,
- cohort member visibility,
- cohort chat,
- tap-out behavior governed by majority approval from active cohort members before a user can leave and join another cohort.

## Near-Term Product Direction

The broader MVP direction described in the product spec includes adding stronger support mechanics around the core flow, especially:

- explicit "need help" alerts,
- push notifications for important cohort activity,
- richer tap-out handling and release workflows,
- deeper in-app accountability loops.

Those features are part of the intended product direction, but the website should still be understood first as a cohort quitting experience, not as a general social network.

## Why This Product Could Matter

Nicotine quitting is often framed as a purely individual discipline problem. NixIt treats it more like a shared challenge. That matters because many users do better when a difficult behavior change is tied to a group identity, a public commitment, and a visible timeline.

In practical terms, NixIt aims to make quitting feel:

- less lonely,
- less abstract,
- more structured,
- more accountable,
- more immediate.

## Simple Product Summary

If you hand this to someone who has never seen NixIt before, the shortest accurate summary is this:

NixIt is a web product for quitting nicotine with other people. Users sign up, choose a shared monthly quit date, join a small cohort, and use a dashboard plus group chat to stay accountable as they quit together. If they want to leave, the cohort must agree to let them out, creating one more chance for the group to help when the user needs it most.
